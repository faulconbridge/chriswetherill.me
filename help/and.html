SQL AND & OR Operators

The AND and OR operators are used in a WHERE clause to specify multiple patterns along which to constrain the results that are returned. The percent sign (%) is used here as a wildcard.

SQL AND/OR Syntax

SELECT column_name(s)
FROM table_name
WHERE column_name1 liKE 'value' AND column_name2 = 'value'

Example

To select words where the length is 6 and the log frequency per million words is between 2 and 3,

SELECT WORD, LENGTH, LgSUBTLWF FROM ELP WHERE LENGTH=6
AND LgSUBTLWF BETWEEN 2 AND 3
